Top 10 Hacks to beautify your LinkedIn Profile

LinkedIn, LinkedIn, LinkedIn!!!!

Why is everyone talking about it so much? Why is it creating a hype?

Because my dear, it is truly important.


To mention specifically about what I like the most about LinkedIn is “Job Search”.


We start our education from kindergarten to primary and secondary schooling, then junior college, and finally graduation. But the ultimatum for these progressive efforts remains the same,


“JOB”


Most tedious task. Easy though, if you have campus placements!


But for others, LinkedIn actually helps you get one, in no time. You can not only see the job listings, but you can also connect with the decision-makers, HRs, CEOs, CTOs of your target company, and start building rapport with them. I don’t need to explain how this would help you, because it is just like creating a referral, even before you actually apply for the job. 


Isn’t it cool?


And that’s not it. LinkedIn is a complete package for an individual to create a personal brand. If you are still confused about what it has for you, you must check out “You are missing a Digital Reputation”. 


Now that you have realized the need for LinkedIn, then definitely you shouldn’t miss out on these TOP 10 COOL HACKS FOR CREATING A BEAUTIFUL LINKEDIN PROFILE. Before that, let me tell you about why it is really important to have a cool and sassy LinkedIn profile?


So, What is a LinkedIn Profile? Is it important actually?


Similar to other social media, LinkedIn users also have a profile, but not exactly similar to other social platforms. LinkedIn is a social platform where your profiles stand as yourself. It is your own personal brand page or website. Creating websites is costly, but building a profile isn’t.

As it is not only a social platform but it is the biggest professional social platform, where more than 100 million users connected are in senior-level or decision making personalities.


Here are some exclusive reasons for why you shouldn’t miss a LinkedIn Profile:

  1. If you've never Google'd yourself, now's the time. What pops up on the first page of the results? Oops, if it’s not your name popping out first, then the LinkedIn profile will help you.
  2. If you are desperately looking for jobs, then a LinkedIn profile will help you create job alerts and also search for job listings. 
  3. Just like companies have their brand, it’s important for you to have your professional brand online. Where do you stand? What is your personal brand value in the market? Why should someone hire you and pay you in 6 figures?
  4. Lastly, Networking! LinkedIn is the greatest networking tool. You not only connect with your colleagues whom you know, but also with industry personnel, decision-makers, for whatever reason you want to connect with them (job or knowledge sharing).



For all the above reasons, think about what if someone visits your profile, what would you show them? Only a blank profile with your name and introduction?


Absolutely not! Of course, It’s your brand page. It has to be attractive and impactful.

If you have realized why you should have a beautiful profile, don’t miss the hacks below:



Top 10 Profile Hacks


  1. Simplified URL


    2. Did you ever imagine having your own website as your individual brand page? But building websites is a tedious task. This is exactly why you should convert your LinkedIn profile to your brand website. Then wait, wouldn’t you need your own customized URL rather than something gibberish?


    The default profile URL contains your name and some irrelevant numbers, which makes it difficult to remember for anyone in your contact. Something like this, would be cool right?


    https://www.linkedin.com/in/roshanpatidar/



    At the top right corner of your profile, you can edit this URL. Otherwise, simply Google it.


  2. Approachable and positive profile photo


    3. As rightly said, “A picture is worth the thousand words”. 

    LinkedIn data shows that the profile with a photo is 27 times more likely to be visited compared to profiles with no photo. 


    The reason behind this is very clear, because according to human nature, we first notice the looks/face before even reading the details. 




    So how should your profile picture be? Certainly not the one which you use on other social media handles. As LinkedIn builds your “Digital Reputation”, it needs to be a simple yet influential one and most importantly it should be your own. It should convey that you are approachable, inviting, competent which can be shown with a fresh smile on your face. 


    Have a perfect headshot, which covers almost 75% of the frame. Avoid fancy selfies or one side photos.


  3. Catchy and influential headline


    4. Headline is something that appears everywhere (e.g. search results, people you may know section, your comments, etc) with your profile photo and your name, even before actually opening the profile. So it should be something that conveys your identity and specialties. By default, it is filled with your current position, but it’s better to make it customized.




    You have 120 characters as the limit, so use it efficiently. Include what you will like people to know about you, your profession, or the services you provide. Include your target audience like, “Helping small businesses to grow with Facebook Ads”. This creates a direct impact.


    Use ‘|’ or ‘,’ separators between the different mentions. E.g. “Entrepreneur | Digital Marketer | Product Manager”.


  4. A story about you in the “About” section 


    5. This “About” section is by far the most important section of your profile, yet the most underestimated. You may have experienced that when you sit for an interview, you are often asked, “Tell me about yourself”. This is the exact section to relate to.


    You should compile this section into your own custom story in such a way that, “what will you like the person to know when given 30 seconds to introduce yourself”. It should be a perfect combination of your personal and professional life, your core competencies, your interests, skills, passion, goals for life, and how you have planned to achieve them and most importantly what makes you different from others.





    Don’t make it too long. Remember 30 seconds!!!  Make it in 3-4 paragraphs, with bullet points about the skills, passion, interest, past life rather than just having your job experience. Dedicate your time to craft your story, because it's worth it.


  5. Show up your skills and let people “Endorse” you


    6. Share the skills that are relevant to you. By doing this you provide credibility to what you have described in your headline, summary, and provides a platform to let other people endorse you. 


    Keep in mind that the skills you mention can be converted into a job or service. And endorsements will prove that you are credible for the mentioned skills. So take time to choose your skills wisely such that people actually endorse you and then it will add value to your profile.


    Before you ask for endorsements, give them! Endorse your connections who deserve it, and ask for yours in return (don’t be shy, it's a barter!!!). You can also select your top 3 skills so that they appear on the top. 


  6. Share your background


    7. Resumes are restricted to 1 to 2 pages. But here you can actually describe your work experience, education, and volunteering experience. You get the freedom to not just describe what you have learned from jobs and experience, but what you have accomplished by it. Describe it in impressive and interesting 4-5 bullet points with good action words.




    The key is to demonstrate the result you have earned and what you can deliver to others if you join them.


  7. Portray your Accomplishments


    8. Accomplishments are not the only ones that you have in your job or education. This section is for you to show your courses, certificates, projects, patents, publications, and awards that you earned. It’s like enriching the skills that you mention by some proof.


    Always show your various test scores, honors and awards, and the organizations that matter to you.


  8. Don’t just ask for a recommendation, give it first …


    9. An endorsement is like giving appreciation to your skill. But recommendation takes it way more ahead. It is a personal testimonial written by others working with you. Don’t just ask for it, first give it to people you worked with and know from the past. Then you can ask for recommendations in return.


    Show the testimonial which is properly elaborating you and your work, but not simply like “Rohan, it was great to work with you”. It should be something like, “Rohan’s contributions to the marketing enabled us to increase the sales by 6% over our original plan”, which will showcase your strengths.


    Quality of the recommendations matter than their quantity. Invest time to craft and display them neatly on your profile.


9) Start growing your network


It seems easy, but we often forget to invite new connections. As you find people related to your field of interest even if they are 2nd or 3rd-degree connections, start connecting with them. This is because widespread connections make you more visible to others. 


LinkedIn had a very healthy community. You can connect with senior-level authorities, and if they find your work interesting, they may end up helping you whenever you need it. Take to visit their profile, before you send them the invite.


It is always recommended to send an invite message along with the connection request before connecting. This helps the person to know where you found them or why would you like to connect, and not just a random connection request.


10) Your connections define you


Having 50 or even fewer connections may lead to many thoughts for the recruiter or the professional like,

1) You know very few people. 

2) You are afraid of talking to a new personality. 

3) Or, you are scared of technology and social media. 


None of those are good for your reputation. That does not mean that you should start connecting randomly with anyone. You should have at least 100-150 connections to whom you are personally connected from the start or the people of your field.


Conclusion


Your LinkedIn profile is a detailed story about you. It is indeed your “Digital Resume”. And surprisingly LinkedIn does allow you to export your profile to your Resume. 


Your profile/resume is like your own website, which showcases your personality even in your absence. So it is very important to make it impactful. Renew your profile with the hacks above and see what miracles it brings to you.

Go grab your optimized profile. You have something very exciting coming your way. All the best!!!
