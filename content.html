Is writing good content enough to rank your website on Google?


Content is believed to be the heart of all marketing strategies. It's not only limited to texts, but also extends to captivating images, dazzling videos and mesmerizing audios.

It is rightly said, ”Everything is CONTENT and content is everywhere”.

Why does one prefer Content Marketing in the beginning?

First fact on which every MSME and small businesses get attracted and are ready to invest time on it is, “Content Marketing is absolutely free”.

Such firms completely rely on organic search traffic to advertise their products & services. Hence it becomes essential for them to rank higher in SERPs (Search Engine Result Pages).

Here the concept of SEO comes into play. SEO (search engine optimization) refers to the process of optimizing your content and website to rank higher in search results for a particular search query or keyword.

Many small businesses and MSMEs believe that such tactics like SEO will bring more traffic to their webpage which may end up generating huge revenue without investing much money on a prior basis.

Now, this ideology is completely accurate. You can convert your huge organic traffic into your potential customers.

No doubt in this assumption, but the amount of effort you will have to put in is insane!

And the bitter truth, there's no guarantee of your content getting ranked for months or for years for some highly competitive keywords such as marketing, entrepreneurship, SEO, technology, Social media, etc.

This traps many of such small firms. Unfortunately, most of these companies end up with a disaster as they don’t have a clear mindset and a proper planning for web-publishings.

Now tell me,

Do you really think that your web-page can get ranked just on the basis of the content you've published?

The genuine answer is, “No, you can NEVER”.

Thousands of blogs get published on the internet for a single keyword every month. You can now guess the intensity of the battle for getting ranked on the top. Every article presents top notch content which almost covers all the aspects of a particular topic.

There's a cut-throat competition going on between thousands of website owners for making it into the top.

Even after generating exclusive brainchilds, puzzling questions and insightful quotes, most of the content remains unnoticed. This only inculcates discouragement and disappointment to the small businesses taking up all their motivation.

Later this demotivation affects their productivity and confidence.

Now we have the following questions to answer,
Why is content not enough to rank your webpage?
Why is content marketing a nightmare for small businesses and startups?

And finally, what factors should you consider to get ranked?

Major potholes in publishing your content

If you want your webpage to rank owing to just ON-page factors, then you have to ensure your content quality, posting frequency and relevance of the content to your services.

Time demanding & hard to rank

The web-article takes a huge amount of time not only for its creation but also to get ranked.

If you are a product based industry willing to generate instant sales, this practice would be a nightmare.

The process of idea generation to getting published involves many steps. Even a small piece of content is a result of several brainstorming sessions, sizzling debates and various rejected ideas.

Thus, for short run businesses, content ranking is hard in its initial phase!!

Requires high amount of skill

One skill you have to majorly look at is being creative and unique. The internet is filled with distinctive content. Hence an improper research may end up in declaring your content plagiarised or unethical.

Again it's very tough to come up with new brainchilds every time. The thought process takes a hell of a time!

Personally, I will recommend MSMEs, which are currently in the beginning phase, to avoid content marketing and focus your attention towards more rapid response strategies which I will talk further in this blog.

Regular postings

In spite of all such difficulties, if you manage to persuade your audience through your writings, the job gets tougher for you further. Your audience craves for content, they demand for more knowledgeable and insightful content.

If you successfully provide them with the required demands, then

Congratulations, you've created a loyal customer base.

But if you fail to do so, you may start losing your audience which would have been your future potential customers.

Having discussed all the loopholes in content presentation, you might have noticed a constant thing. If you belong to a well established firm, which has a praiseworthy domain authority and a quality web network, you can easily cope up with these drawbacks.

However, the sufferers are the small startups and MSMEs. Lack of manpower, capital and experience hinders their progress.

SEO fails to help them due to their poor site structure, domain authority and web-networking.

Why is it difficult to rank just on the basis of the content you write?

Even if you choose a pleasant topic, manage to do a perfect keyword research and optimize your content accordingly, you may not get ranked.

That's the bitter truth.

Thousands of blogs get published on the internet just for a single keyword every month. You can now guess the intensity of the battle for getting ranked on the top. Every article presents top notch content which almost covers all the aspects of a particular topic.

Let's understand this with an example. When you search “ SEO ” in Google search query, you will encounter the following organic search results.

Have a look, the first and third search result. Surprised!!
Google is ranking third for this keyword while Moz being top ranked. Isn't that strange?

If you go through the content of Moz as well as Google , the content written by Google always outstands Moz in every manner. Even the domain authority of Google being greater than Moz .
(Domain Score (DS) of Google:100 DS of Moz :92)

This indeed proves that you cannot rank just on the basis of content. The site technicalities also matters equally. These factors are called OFF-page factors for SEO.

The Google ranking algorithm also considers these technical factors while crawling your website. Afterall, Google also has to take care of user experience while analyzing and ranking your webpage.

These technical factors are often called OFF-page SEO. So let's peep into these essentials and some of the must do things to improve your site rankings.

Link building

Link building is a process of linking one blog with another blog, newsfeed or other informative resources. Google crawlers identify these links as a source of information. Hence more the number of links, more informative the blog is.

These links create a web like structure of web pages which are linked to each other.

Internal linking/crosslinking

Internal linking refers to linking your own content in a blog to build a web like structure. Internal link ensures your site authority and helps in building your domain score. It's basically for audience reference.

External linking

Here you are linking to your competitors website. Some believe it to be an abstract idea to link to an external site. However most of the sites use this to get a backlink. Backlink basically refers to getting linked back (analogous to getting a follow back on instagram). It creates a web like structure across many blogs to create an info-web link.

Social Media linking

Google crawlers also look at the amount of social shares. The more social shares you get, the more is the possibility of your site getting a high rank. This will also build your engagement on social media and increase the reach of your content. Look at the following image, these highly ranked sites also have an ample of social shares.

Technical Factors of site

Site optimization
User experience matters! While developing your site, take care of text and image processings, loading time, site optimization for mobile users and many other things. Google cares about its users. Hence it ranks the sites that provide the best user experience.
Dwell time
Dwell times is the time a user spends on your website. Greater the dwell time higher your site gets ranked.

Don't believe me, Here's an example,

Rand Fishkin conducted an experiment where he asked his users to spend more time on this site rather than the highly ranked one. You will be surprised to know that within 70 minutes of the experiment, his site jumped from fourth position to the number ONE ranked site!

Surveys

Surveys are the best way to improve your site. Small surveys with your audience helps not only in improving yourself but also gaining a mutual trust with your readers. You can use some softwares for running some surveys on your webpage.

Videos and Podcasts

Suppose you write a blog on a particular topic which is a bit long. It's natural that people will never read the whole blog resulting in decreased dwell time. Now, what if you post a video along with the blog which explains the same content as that in the blog. The readers will surely jump on the video part increasing your brand value. Moreover these videos will also help in your personal branding and personality improvement. Podcasts often attract a huge crowd. Folks love to watch their favourite influencer/ inspiration sharing his thoughts.

All these strategies help you in building site authority and increases your outreach.

Is there any other alternative for content marketing?

You have done great in SEO, good, but what next. We have to focus on some strategies apart from content marketing. This seems to be a different concept but indirectly links to building your brand and ranking your site!

Black-Hat SEO

Surprised! Black hat seo is considered to be an illegal way for ranking your site. Keyword spamming, link stuffing are some techniques that fall into this category. However it helps your site to come in the top of SERPs for an ample amount of time.

This is the best practise recommended for short run businesses who target to sell their product in a short interval of time and scrap it afterwards. It will indeed rank your site for a small while and help you reach a maximum number of audience.

Also small firms will come into limelight for a while and can achieve a particular number of audience. Later they can work on fair practices to rank their site organically (White-Hat SEO ).

marketing

The most amazing thing about Social media platforms is their user base. Billions of people surf through a variety of platforms searching for things of their interest. With a proper planning, you can develop your follower base.

As a small business, this site helps you to reach the required customer in a much shorter period of time. Your bio should contain your website, so that people will reach you there for any enquiry.

Youtube video content

The top searched entertainment website, YouTube, has some really useful & engaging video content. Visual content always captures more eye balls than prolonged & interesting texts. You can again share your web pages link in the description box or comment section.

Google also considers such redirects from any social platform and helps you for gaining an enormous customer base and a higher search rank.

Conclusion

Hope you get the key concepts behind this blog. Just writing good content is an incomplete process which hits only half of the milestone. You have to consider several technical aspects while publishing a web-article.

Google crawler is ultimately a robot, it doesn't have any feelings. It won't get influenced by your poignant content. What matters for it is the user experience, quality and quantity of information. Hence one should equally focus on both the aspects of a web article.

No one knows the actual google algorithms which are behind this crawling process. But one thing I'm definitely sure about is that a smooth user-experience and quality content will help you in.

Small businesses may find it difficult at beginning to establish their audience base via organic search but there should be no scope of getting discouraged and disappointed. Establishing a good domain authority takes time. Your effort will surely generate fruitful results.
